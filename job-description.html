Registered Veterinary Technician (RVT) – Job Description


Position: Registered Veterinary Technician (RVT)
Reports to: Lead RVT / Practice Manager
Location: Chaos & Paws Veterinary Care
Address: 1 Georgian Drive, Barrie, ON
Employment Type: Full-time / Part-time

Position Overview:

The Registered Veterinary Technician (RVT) plays a crucial role in providing high-quality patient care, assisting veterinarians in medical procedures, and ensuring efficient clinic operations. This position requires technical expertise, strong communication skills, and a commitment to patient well-being and client education.

Key Responsibilities:

Patient Care & Medical Support

  • Assist veterinarians in examinations, diagnostics, and medical procedures.
  • Perform venipuncture, IV catheter placement, and administer medications as directed.
  • Monitor anesthesia and assist in surgical procedures, ensuring patient safety.
  • Provide nursing care, including post-operative monitoring and critical care support.
  • Collect and analyze laboratory samples (blood, urine, feces, cytology, etc.).

Client Communication & Education

  • Communicate with pet owners regarding treatment plans, medications, and post-care instructions.
  • Educate clients on preventive care, nutrition, and general pet health.
  • Handle client concerns with professionalism and empathy.

Clinic Operations & Team Collaboration

  • Maintain accurate medical records and input data into practice management software.
  • Ensure proper handling and storage of pharmaceuticals and controlled substances.
  • Maintain a clean, organized, and sterile work environment.
  • Collaborate with veterinarians, RVTs, veterinary assistants, and reception staff to ensure seamless clinic workflow.
  • Follow safety protocols and infection control measures.

Technical & Administrative Duties

  • Take and process digital radiographs, ensuring proper positioning and safety measures.
  • Assist in dental cleanings, extractions, and oral health assessments.
  • Prepare and maintain surgical equipment and instruments.

Qualifications & Requirements:

  • Graduate of an accredited Veterinary Technology program.
  • Registered (or eligible for registration) with the Ontario Association of Veterinary Technicians (OAVT).
  • Minimum 1 year of experience preferred.
  • Strong technical skills, including anesthesia monitoring, lab work, and radiography.
  • Excellent communication and teamwork abilities.
  • Ability to lift up to 50 lbs and remain on feet for extended periods.
  • Comfort handling a variety of patients, including anxious, aggressive, or unpredictable animals.

Work Environment & Expectations:

  • Maintain a high level of professionalism, ensuring a positive and respectful workplace.
  • Exhibit patience, adaptability, and problem-solving skills in a fast-paced environment.
  • Uphold the clinic’s standards of care and customer service.
  • Foster a culture of learning and continuous professional development.

Compensation & Benefits:

  • Competitive salary based on experience.
  • Paid overtime and statutory holidays.
  • Health and dental benefits.
  • Continuing education (CE) allowance.
  • Uniform allowance.
  • Supportive team environment with opportunities for professional growth.

Employee Manual Policies:

  • Drug and Alcohol Abuse Policy: We maintain a drug-free workplace. Substance use that impairs job performance or safety is strictly prohibited.
  • Sexual and Psychological Harassment Policy: Harassment of any kind will not be tolerated. We promote a safe and respectful work environment.
  • Noncompete Agreements: Employees may be required to sign a noncompete agreement to protect the clinic’s business interests.
  • Jury Duty: Employees called for jury duty will be given the necessary time off as required by law.
  • Training Procedures: All new employees undergo structured training to ensure competency in clinic protocols and medical procedures.
  • Safety Procedures: Employees are expected to follow all safety guidelines to prevent injury and ensure a secure working environment.
  • Probationary Period: New hires will undergo a 3 months probationary period during which performance and fit within the team will be assessed.
  • Termination Procedures: Employment may be terminated in accordance with clinic policies, ensuring fairness and professionalism.
